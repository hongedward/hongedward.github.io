5. DEATH PARADE
PRODUCTION
Studio: Madhouse Year: 2015 Episodes: 12
Director: Yuzuru Tachikawa Music: Yuuki Hayashi Character Design: Shinichi Kurita
Genres: drama, mystery, psychological, supernatural
MAIN CAST
Decim "Black-haired Woman" Nona Clavis
CV: Tomoaki Maeno CV: Asami Seto CV: Rumi Ookubo CV: Kouki Uchiyama
Ginti Mayu Oculus Quin
CV: Yoshimasa Hosoya CV: Atsumi Tanezaki CV: Tessho Genda CV: Ryoko Shiraishi
SYNOPSIS
"Humans all behave the same way, like idiots. They all forget that someday, they're gonna die, so the moment they come face to face with death, they cling to life." -Ginti

After humans die, they are sent to either Heaven or Hell. But sometimes, the destination they deserve is not so clear. Such morally ambiguous souls are sent to Quindecim, a bar that exists between the worlds of the living and the dead. There, humans play a game with their lives on the line under the watchful eyes of the bartender Decim, who draws out the players' true natures in order to decide who will be reincarnated and who will be sent to the void.
REVIEW
Not sure if you're interested? Watch the original OVA, Death Billiards, first.

4. PSYCHO-PASS
PRODUCTION
Studio: I.G. Production Year: 2012-2013 Episodes: 22 Original Creator: Gen Urobuchi
Director: Katsuyuki Motohiro Music: Yugo Kanno Original Design: Akira Amano Character Design: Kyoji Asano
Genres: action, mystery, psychological, science fiction, thriller
MAIN CAST
Akane Tsunemori Shinya Kogami Shogo Makishima Nobuchika Ginoza
CV: Kana Hanazawa CV: Tomokazu Seki CV: Takahiro Sakurai CV: Kenji Nojima
Shusei Kagari Tomomi Masaoka Yayoi Kunizuka Shion Karanomori
CV: Akira Ishida CV: Kinryu Arimoto CV: Shizuka Itou CV: Miyuki Sawashiro
SYNOPSIS
"I think the only time people really have value is when they act according to their own will." -Shogo Makishima

The year is 2112. Japan is governed by the Sibyl System, a supercomputer that can instantly measure a person's "crime coefficient" (their capacity and desire to commit crime). The police, who act on Sibyl's orders, are split into two groups: Enforcers, latent criminals employed to hunt their own kind, and Inspectors, supervisors who keep Enforcers in line. Each officer is issued a Dominator, a gun that measures the target's crime coefficient at gunpoint - the safety is unlocked only if the coefficient is sufficiently high. Psycho-Pass follows the cases of rookie Inspector Akane Tsunemori and vengeful Enforcer Shinya Kogami.
REVIEW
Edgy, philosophical, and one hell of a ride.

3. STEINS;GATE
PRODUCTION
Studio: White Fox Year: 2011 Episodes: 24 Original Creators: 5pb., Nitroplus
Directors: Hiroshi Hamasaki,
Takuya Sato		 Music: Takeshi Abo,
Jun Murakami		 Original Design: huke Character Design: Kyuta Sakai
Genres: drama, mystery, psychological, science fiction, thriller
MAIN CAST
Rintaro Okabe Mayuri Shiina Kurisu Makise Itaru Hashida
CV: Mamoru Miyano CV: Kana Hanazawa CV: Asami Imai CV: Tomokazu Seki
Suzuha Amane Moeka Kiryu Faris "Nyan-nyan" Ruka Urushibara
CV: Yukari Tamura CV: Saori Goto CV: Halko Momoi CV: Yu Kobayashi
SYNOPSIS
"History dictates that it is the wise who are most foolish."
-Rintaro Okabe

Self-proclaimed mad scientist and broke college student Rintaro "Okarin" Okabe runs the Future Gadget Laboratory in his one-room apartment with his roommate, hacker-otaku Itaru "Daru" Hashida. When the two accidentally invent a microwave that can send text messages to the past, their lab becomes the center of attention for a growing group of friends. A desperate struggle to alter history ensues after the lab and its members become targeted by a malevolent organization in pursuit of time travel technology.
REVIEW
The time-travel genre done right.

2. FULLMETAL ALCHEMIST: BROTHERHOOD
PRODUCTION
Studio: Bones Year: 2009-2010 Episodes: 64 Original Creator: Hiromu Arakawa
Director: Yasuhiro Irie Music: Akira Senju Original Design: Hiromu Arakawa Character Design: Hiroki Kanno
Genres: action, adventure, drama, fantasy, mystery
MAIN CAST
Edward Elric Alphonse Elric Winry Rockbell Roy Mustang
CV: Romi Park CV: Rie Kugimiya CV: Megumi Takamoto CV: Shinichiro Miki
Scar Lin Yao Van Hohenheim
CV: Kenta Miyake CV: Mamoru Miyano CV: Unshou Ishizuka
SYNOPSIS
"Humankind cannot gain anything without first giving something in return. To obtain, something of equal value must be lost. That is Alchemy's first law of Equivalent Exchange. In those days, we really believed that to be the world's one and only truth."
-Alphonse Elric

Mankind has harnessed the power of alchemy, the scientific art of manipulating matter with natural energy. As children, brothers Edward and Alphonse Elric attempted the alchemic taboo of Human Transmutation, believing they could ressurect their deceased mother, and paid dearly for their naiveté. Now, Ed has automail prothetics for his right arm and left leg, and Al's soul is bound to a large suit of armor. To reclaim their original bodies, the Elric brothers embark on a journey to find the legendary Philosopher's Stone, a mysterious artifact rumored to bypass the law of Equivalent Exchange.
REVIEW
The epitome of Shonen anime.

1. BAKEMONOGATARI
PRODUCTION
Studio: Shaft Year: 2009 Episodes: 15 Original Creator: Nisio Isin
Director: Akiyuki Shinbo Music: Satoru Kosaki Original Design: Vofan Character Design: Akio Watanabe
Genres: comedy, drama, mystery, romance, supernatural
MAIN CAST
Koyomi Araragi Hitagi Senjougahara Tsubasa Hanekawa Mayoi Hachikuji
CV: Hiroshi Kamiya CV: Chiwa Saito CV: Yui Horie CV: Emiri Kato
Suruga Kanbaru Nadeko Sengoku Meme Oshino
CV: Miyuki Sawashiro CV: Kana Hanazawa CV: Takahiro Sakurai
SYNOPSIS
"People have to save themselves. One person saving another is impossible." -Meme Oshino

High school senior Koyomi Araragi just experienced a hellish Spring Break in which he was turned into a vampire. Having reclaimed most of his humanity with the help of wandering excorcist Meme Oshino, Araragi encounters five girls who have also been afflicted by Kaii (supernatural oddities): Senjougahara, whose weightlessness defies physics; Hachikuji, who can never find her way home; Kanbaru, who has been cursed by a Monkey's Paw; Sengoku, who's being strangled by an invisible snake; and Hanekawa, whose body is possessed by a demonic cat. One by one, Araragi learn of their problems, their stories, and their secrets.
REVIEW
Top-notch storytelling with verbose characters.
